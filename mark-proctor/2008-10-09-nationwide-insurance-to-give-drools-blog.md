### [Nationwide Insurance to give Drools Blog](<https://blog.kie.org/2008/10/nationwide-insurance-to-give-drools-blog.html>)

by [Mark Proctor](<https://blog.kie.org/category/all?search_authors=3> "Posts by Mark Proctor") \- October 9, 2008  
[Rules](<https://blog.kie.org/category/rules>) [Article](<https://blog.kie.org/content_type/article>)

GoogleAlert just let me know that [Nationwide](<http://www.nationwide.com/index.jsp>)  
are doing a Drools presentation at the [Central Ohio Java Users Group](<http://www.cojug.org/>) on the 14th of October. The details can be found [here](<http://www.cojug.org/index.php?option=com_eventlist&Itemid=27&func=details&did=42>), and I’ve pasted them below. It’s really nice to see other members in the community promoting the use of Drools, especially a big brand name like Nationwide:

Event  
---  
when:|  10/14/2008 | 11:30 – 12:30|   
Event title| JBoss (Drools) Rules Engine in J2EE Enterprise Applications  
Where:|  [ OCLC’s Kilgour building private dinning room](<http://www.oclc.org/about/headquarters/maps/>) – Dublin  
Category:|  [2008 Meetings](<http://www.cojug.org/index.php?option=com_eventlist&Itemid=27&func=shcatev1&categid=6>)  
**Abstract** Provides a glossary view of rules engine. Some comparison between BRMS and Traditional Rules Engine (embedded). A quick look at JBoss Rules, different ways to represent the rules and some highlights of the overall programming environment.**Bio** Name: Keith Ebare  
JobTitle: Consulting, IT Architect  
Company: Nationwide P&C  
Technical Area of Interest: J2EE and usage/applicability of open source productsName: Anu Alwar  
Job Title: Consulting IT Architect  
Company: Nationwide P&CImplementation of rules engines for externalizing business rules in a j2ee application.  
Masters in Computer Science from Ohio State University. About 13 years of IT experience with about 10 in j2ee.
